Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our car rental services in Kenya.

What documents do I need to rent a car in Kenya?

You need a valid driving license, passport or national ID, and a credit card for the security deposit. International visitors require a valid passport and an international driving permit.

Is insurance included in the rental price?

Yes, all our rentals include basic insurance coverage. Additional comprehensive insurance options are available for extra protection.

Can I drive the rental car to neighboring countries?

Cross-border travel is possible with prior arrangement and additional documentation. Please contact us at least 48 hours before your trip to arrange this.

What happens if the car breaks down?

We provide 24/7 roadside assistance throughout Kenya. In case of a breakdown, contact our emergency number immediately for prompt support.

Are there mileage restrictions?

Most of our rentals come with unlimited mileage within Kenya. Special rates apply for long-distance travel to remote areas.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept all major credit cards, mobile money payments (M-Pesa, Airtel Money), and bank transfers. Cash payments are also accepted at our offices.

What are your booking policies?

Free cancellation up to 24 hours before pickup. No hidden charges. Pay via M-Pesa, Credit/Debit Cards, Bank Transfer. Driver available at +KES 2,000/day. Long-term rentals available with monthly discounts.
